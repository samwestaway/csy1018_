  • Web Designer with 34education     - In this role you’ll be working on our mid to high-level projects, of which all are fully responsive. These projects are powered by our very own CMS and in-house responsive framework. You will need extensive knowledge of designing fully responsive websites, frameworks and methods including up-to-date good practice and knowledge of modern design conventions.

    This position is situated in the Design Studio working alongside a mixed team of junior, mid and senior designers. You will also have access to the latest Adobe Creative Cloud Suite using the best iMac/MacBook and Wacom creative drawing tablet kit.

    A minimum of 2-3 years professional experience working with web, print and branding. Strong technological skills, e.g. high level of knowledge of Adobe Creative Suite, a good level of knowledge of HTML/CSS, an understanding of UX. A proven knowledge of typography, layout, colour and grid structure. Ability to communicate effectively. Demonstrable ability to manage your own workload and manage your time effectively.

    Salary: Competitive (Depending on experience) Flexibility for a work life balance Great creative office environment Excellent employee facilities Other than salary, the following benefits commence on successful completion of the probation period: Contributory Pension Scheme, Private Medical, Additional optional benefits.


  • Junior Web Tester     - You’ll be working with various people across the business from designers to developers to product specialists to ensure that every piece of the puzzle is in place before we unleash new content and functionality onto our sites.

    You will need to be a “self-starter” with excellent communication and time management skills, general technical knowledge and the desire to grow their skills and involvement within the business. Strong problem-solving skills and a methodical approach are also essential.

    Plan and carry out functional, integration, and end-to-end testing of desktop, mobile, and tablet applications. Raise defects using JIRA as our defect-tracking tool. Plan and execute manual integration, regression, browser compatibility tests. Execute ad-hoc functional testing on request and providing sign-off. Report test results to the QA lead and Project Manager. Working with the wider team to develop automated testing processes Managing bug tracking software and assignment of tasks along with priority levels. Excellent communication skills as you will be communicating directly with clients on a day-to-day basis, face-to-face, phone and screen share. Effectively communicate issues and concerns to developers, designers, IT and other business areas.


  • Junior CSS Developer     - Work closely with the content planners to influence the design of content and ensure that plans are technically achievable. Work closely with colleagues in the online content, merchandising and marketing teams to ensure a consistent customer message across all channels, lead and co-ordinate asset creation and content planning for the build and running of our websites, strive to identify new and creative ways to drive and improve customer experience, clearly and persuasively communicate designs and concepts to senior management, develop and test page layouts and asset creation to enhance customers’ online experience, support supplier revenue and ensure that SEO best practice is applied, be willing and able to test new website functionality that impacts content / design, create engaging graphics across digital channels (web, mobile)

    Required skills include HTML (intermediate), CSS skills (intermediate), experience with web development, comfortable with design and prototyping tools, adobe Photoshop, knowledge of user experience best-practice.

  • The Web Designer job with 34education requires you to have knowledge within HTML and CSS, Adobe Products, experience in web-development and an effective understanding of the methods for good practice. This shares that similarity with the Junior CSS developer, whilst it does not require this in Web Tester. The similarities between the three are that they all require the ability to effectively communicate with others. All three jobs require you to have an understanding of how the HTML and CSS interact as the design and developer jobs will need you to actually implement the code, the tester will still need to have the understanding of how this works as when they find errors/problems they will be required to submit a formal report to the developers to allow the changes to be rectified easily.

    The average salary for these 3 jobs is similar in the range of: £18,000 up to £25,000 depending on current experience and qualifications. As the second and third are junior jobs there is a high chance of working your way up through the business. Whereas the first job is looking for a semi-experienced applicant that will be taking a mid-level job position but could assist with developing juniors and potentially earn extra bonuses for doing so.
